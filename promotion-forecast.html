Why Category Results May Not Match the Category Objective

Merchant-facing explanation of total-plan optimization and category rollups.

Short Answer

This optimiser is designed to make the best total plan for the store / price area, not to independently maximise every category. It chooses one offer per item-week slot and evaluates the full promotional plan against the selected objective: Units, Revenue, or AGP.

What Is Being Optimized

Category is a reporting and governance lens. The optimization target is the total selected plan across the configured store / price-area scope.

  • Each item-week slot has candidate offers: different depths, tactics, channels, or no-promo options.
  • The solver picks exactly one candidate per slot.
  • Units, Revenue, and AGP are three complete full-plan scenarios, not category rows.

Why A Category Can Look Unbalanced

The model may lean into categories where predicted response, margin, funding, price image, and guardrail compliance are strongest. Another category may receive less investment if promotion would weaken the total plan.

How To Read The Rows

The Units row is the plan selected when movement is primary; its Revenue and AGP columns are outcomes of that Units-focused full plan. Sometimes that plan can also produce strong AGP because those items have good margin, funding, or response.

What Would Change This

If the business wants category balance, the model needs explicit category-level constraints: AGP floors, promo-share caps, investment budgets, or minimum representation rules.
